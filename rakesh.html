FAVOURITE CRICKETER

ROHIT SHARMA

Rohit Gurunath Sharma (born 30 April 1987) is an Indian cricketer and the captain of India national cricket team in Test and ODI formats. Widely regarded as one of the best batsmen of his generation and one of the greatest opening batters of all time, he is a right-handed batsman who plays for India in international cricket, Mumbai Indians in IPL and for Mumbai in domestic cricket. Rohit previously captained India in all three formats, and after leading the team to victory in 2024 T20 World Cup, he announced his retirement from T20Is.

He has received two national honours, the Arjuna Award in 2015 and the prestigious Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award in 2020 by the Government of India. Under his captaincy, India won the 2018 Asia Cup and the 2023 Asia Cup and 2024 T20 worldcup, the seventh and eighth time the country won the title, both in ODI format as well as the 2018 Nidahas Trophy, their second overall and first in T20I format.

ACHIEVEMENTS

    International Career

  • ODI Double Centuries: Rohit is the only player to score three double centuries in One Day Internationals (ODIs).
  • Highest Individual ODI Score: He holds the record for the highest individual score in an ODI with 264 runs against Sri Lanka in 2014.
  • Most Centuries in World Cups: Rohit is the only player to score five centuries in a single Cricket World Cup edition (2019).
  • Most Sixes in International Cricket: He surpassed Chris Gayle's record for the most international sixes.
  • ODI Player of the Year: Awarded by the ICC in 2019.

    • T20 Internationals

  • Most Runs for India: Rohit is the second leading run-scorer for India in T20 Internationals.
  • First Indian to Score a T20I Century as Captain: He is the only Indian to score a century in T20Is as a captain.
  • Most Sixes by an Indian: Holds the record for the most sixes by an Indian in T20Is.

    • Domestic and IPL Career

  • IPL Captaincy: Rohit has captained Mumbai Indians to five IPL titles (2013, 2015, 2017, 2019, and 2020), making him the most successful captain in IPL history.
  • Most Runs in IPL: He is one of the top run-scorers in the history of the Indian Premier League (IPL).

    • Team Achievements

  • Asia Cup Wins: Under his captaincy, India won the Asia Cup in 2018 and 2023.
  • T20 World Cup: Led India to victory in the 2024 T20 World Cup and announced his retirement from T20Is following the win.
